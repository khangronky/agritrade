Issue:

Economic Disadvantages
Lower prices: Farmers selling through middlemen received 15% lower prices compared to those selling directly to wholesalers 
Reduced gross profit: Farmers using middlemen earned significantly less.
Commission costs: While farmers don't directly pay commissions, middlemen receive commission from wholesalers, which may influence the prices offered to farmers 
Market Structure Issues
Longer marketing channels: Intermediation creates longer value chains, reducing the percentage of marketing margin captured by farmers 
Lock-in effects: Farmers may not benefit from new market opportunities due to being tied to middlemen relationships 
Bargaining power imbalance: Middlemen have strong bargaining positions due to their superior knowledge of supply and demand conditions 
Contractual Constraints
Pre-agreed pricing: 97% of farmers using middlemen had pre-agreed prices, potentially limiting their ability to benefit from favorable market conditions 


Cause:

Social Network Factors
Ethnic and Religious Ties: Farmers with ethnic or religious connections to middlemen are significantly more likely to use them as trading partners. These social bonds create trust and facilitate economic exchange in rural communities.
Age and Experience: Older farmers prefer middlemen because they're more embedded in social networks and have established personalized relationships. 
Resource Constraints
Limited Assets: Farmers with fewer livestock units and smaller farm sizes are more likely to use middlemen. Resource-poor farmers lack the capacity to deal directly with wholesaler requirements.
Transportation: Farmers without carts or transportation assets depend on middlemen to reach markets.
Financial Dependency: Farmers often rely on middlemen for loans, seeds, and fertilizers. This creates a binding relationship where farmers are obligated to sell to them, even at lower prices.
Immediate Cash Needs: Farmers often face urgent financial pressures (e.g., daily consumption, debt repayment), pushing them to sell quickly to middlemen who can provide immediate payment rather than waiting for better prices.
Market Access Benefits
Guaranteed Sales: Middlemen provide market access for low-resource farmers who might otherwise struggle to find buyers. They reduce the risk of not finding buyers for entire harvests.
Risk Avoidance Behavior: Farmers prefer stable but lower income over uncertain but higher returns, using middlemen as a way to minimize market risk.
Reduced Transaction Costs: Middlemen handle search, negotiation, and quality assessment, saving farmers time and effort in market transactions.
Location Disadvantage: Farmers in remote areas rely more heavily on middlemen due to distance from main trading hubs .


=> Despite earning 225% lower profits, these factors explain why 68% of farmers continue using middlemen.
